# Competitive Response

A Claude skill that develops a clear-headed strategic response to a competitor's move or a market threat. When a rival changes pricing, launches a product, repositions, or starts winning your deals, it helps you decide what to actually do about it.

## What it does

When a competitor moves, the instinct is to react, and the reaction is usually disproportionate to the threat. This skill puts a clear head between the move and the response. It interprets what the competitor is actually doing and why, sizes the threat honestly rather than catastrophically, maps the full range of responses including the discipline of doing nothing, and recommends a course with the reasoning visible so you can challenge it.

## How it works

It runs an eight-part analysis: interpreting the move's intent, sizing the threat across three horizons, mapping the full response spectrum from ignore through leapfrog, analysing each viable option for cost and reversibility, finding asymmetric responses the competitor cannot easily match, working out what sales and customers need to hear, landing a recommendation with success metrics and a contingency, and lifting up to long-term positioning.

Before it analyses anything, it probes extensively across the key factors, the move, the competitor's nature, the market reaction, your real strengths and vulnerabilities, the stakes, and the history, drawing them out through focused questioning rather than demanding a filled-in template. The probing is deliberate, because a shallow analysis built on your first three sentences will sound authoritative while resting on air. Its most valuable discipline is then resisting the overreaction: it gives "ignore" and "monitor" real weight, because many competitive moves are best met with nothing or with watchful waiting, and an advisor who always recommends action is just reacting on your behalf.

## When to use it

Reach for it when a competitor makes a major move, during competitive deal losses, or proactively during planning. It triggers on situations like "a competitor just dropped their price, how do we respond", "we keep losing deals to X", or "help me think through our competitive position".

## Structure

- `SKILL.md` — the advisor behaviour and the discipline against overreaction
- `references/analysis-framework.md` — the full eight-part analysis with guidance on doing each step well

## Installation

Package the folder as a `.skill` file and install it through the skills interface in your Claude client.
