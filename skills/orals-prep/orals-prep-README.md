# Orals Prep

A Claude skill that prepares a team to win the oral presentation stage of a competitive bid, from message and structure through speaker assignment, Q&A handling, and mock-orals rehearsal. It can play the mock evaluation panel itself.

## What it does

Orals are where bids are won and lost in person. After the written proposals are scored, the top few bidders present to the evaluation panel, and that presentation often carries a meaningful share of the technical score, enough to move a team from second place to first or from finalist to loser. The written proposal describes capability; orals demonstrate it, face to face, under questioning. This skill prepares a team to win that room: the message, the structure, the speakers, the answers to the questions they fear, and the rehearsal that turns a competent team into a winning one.

## How it works

It establishes the format, rules, panel, and criteria first, because the client sets the rules and breaching them can disqualify. Then it builds the presentation to complement the written proposal rather than read it back, structured on the Triple-S formula (state, support, summarise) and led by differentiators and the client's hot buttons. It assigns speakers with a matrix that routes each section and question type to the right voice, giving the proposed key personnel real speaking parts since the panel is assessing the actual team.

The heart of it is Q&A and rehearsal, because that is where orals are most often decided. It drills the question types the panel will use, clarifications, challenges, comparisons, and risk probes, including the single toughest question the team is dreading, trains a clean answer-first-support-confirm technique, and coaches honest handling of genuine weaknesses without going defensive or contradicting the proposal.

It then runs Mock Orals as a formal review gate, the orals equivalent of a written Red Team review: an independent panel scores the rehearsal exactly as the client's source selection board would, across content, delivery, and Q&A, against the oral evaluation criteria, and runs a full four-phase process from preparation through live observation, a tough Q&A simulation, a consensus debrief, and a scored report with prioritised fixes, owners, and deadlines. The skill can play that mock panel itself, scoring honestly rather than reassuringly, since a flattering mock is the one thing that guarantees a worse performance on the day.

## When to use it

Reach for it when a team has reached or is preparing for orals, shortlist interviews, an oral defence, or any live presentation to an evaluation panel. It triggers on requests like "help us prepare for orals", "we've been shortlisted and have to present", "run a mock orals session", "what questions will they ask and how do we answer", or "coach our team for the presentation".

## Structure

- `SKILL.md` — the prep approach across format, presentation, speakers, Q&A, and mock orals
- `references/orals-intake.md` — the questions to establish the format, panel, criteria, and team first
- `references/presentation-and-qa.md` — the Triple-S structure and the full Q&A playbook including weakness and hostile-premise handling
- `references/mock-orals-gate.md` — the formal four-phase Mock Orals review gate, run as an independent evaluator simulation

## Installation

Package the folder as a `.skill` file and install it through the skills interface in your Claude client.
